Healthcare Information Technology (Healthcare IT)    

The Health Insurance sector has undergone major changes in quality and sophistication

over the past several years. Numerous factors have contributed towards these improvements

that have greatly enhanced the overall Provider and Patient experience in healthcare.

Key among them is the increasing role and use of IT in managing the complex business

processes and workflows. The healthcare industry has seen new standards implementations

for privacy, ASC X12 4010A1 transaction sets, security, National Employer Identifier and

National Provider Identifier.

 

Most recently Congress passed into law two new standards: Updating electronic standards

from 4010A1 to 5010 and conversion of ICD-9 codes to ICD-10. By the extended deadline

March 31, 2012 all covered entities must be able to send and receive all HIPAA transactions

in the 5010 format and by the new deadline October 1, 2014 all covered entities must be

compliant with ICD-10 codes. Given the dramatic code changes under ICD-10,

healthcare organizations (Payer and Providers) must consider both business and the

technology requirements needed.

ICD-10 Impact Assessment and Remediation:


The upgrade/migration from the ICD-9 to ICD-10 codes will likely be one of the most extensive

healthcare conversion projects that healthcare organizations will undertake. The vast majority of

IT systems and many healthcare policies will be affected. Many healthcare payers and providers

are facing difficulty in meeting CMS deadline for transitioning from ICD-9 to ICD-10. Many

organization do not have the skilled resources needed to perform impact analysis, remediation

plans and testing because of its complex mapping requirements.

 

ALG Technology Solutions provides full life cycle services that support each phase of an

ICD-10 upgrade / implementation project.

 

ICD-10 Impact Analysis

Our experience team will perform an ICD-9 usage Analysis to assess and report systems that are

impacted in your workflow, ICD-9 codes are being used and documents (design) impacted. Based

on this Impact assessment we assist our clients in the planning and development of ICD-10 detail

remediation plans including financial risks.
 

ICD-10 Remediation Support

We support our clients ICD-10 remediation efforts according to their approach and needs. In general,

healthcare providers and payers are considering the following options to make their systems

ICD-10 compliant.

  • A complete replacement of their existing systems with new 5010 and ICD-10 compliant systems;
  • Performing an Impact Assessment on their existing systems followed by the full remediation; or
  • Performing an Impact Assessment and the use of Step Up/Step down tools.
